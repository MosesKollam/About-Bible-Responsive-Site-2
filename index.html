About Bible:

The Bible — A Living Book of Divine Truth

The Bible is more than a book — it is a divine library, a spiritual guide, and a historical record that has transformed billions of lives across centuries. Revered as the Word of God, it reveals God’s nature, His will for humanity, and His plan of redemption through Jesus Christ.

Structure of the Bible:

The Bible contains 66 books, divided into two major parts:

The Old Testament (39 books)
  • Written primarily in Hebrew (with some Aramaic).
  • Focuses on God’s covenant with Israel, the creation of the world, the law, history, poetry, and prophecy.
  • Key Sections:
    • The Pentateuch (Genesis to Deuteronomy) — The Law of Moses.
    • Historical Books (Joshua to Esther) — Israel’s history.
    • Poetry & Wisdom (Job to Song of Solomon) — Worship and human experience.
    • Prophets (Isaiah to Malachi) — Warnings and promises from God.
The New Testament (27 books)
  • Written in Greek.
  • Focuses on the life, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ, and the growth of the early Church.
  • Key Sections:
    • The Gospels (Matthew to John) — Accounts of Jesus’ life and teachings.
    • Acts of the Apostles — The early Church’s history.
    • Epistles (Romans to Jude) — Letters to believers and churches.
    • Revelation — A prophetic vision of the end times.

Central Theme: God’s Redemption

The Bible’s core message is redemption — how God created the world, how mankind fell into sin, and how God provides a way back to Him through Jesus Christ.

Genesis shows the beginning of creation and humanity.

Exodus to Malachi reveals God's relationship with Israel.

The Gospels unveil the arrival of the Messiah — Jesus.

The New Testament Epistles guide the Church.
